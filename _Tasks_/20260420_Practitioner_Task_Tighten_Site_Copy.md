# Tighten practitioner.digital Site Copy

## Objective
Tighten the copy across `practitioner.digital` so it reads more clearly, more professionally, and more quickly.

The current site contains too much text that feels directed inward or autobiographical. We want the writing to become:
- tighter
- more employer/client-facing
- more production-aware
- easier to scan
- less poetic where that hurts clarity

This is a **copy tightening task**, not a visual redesign task.

## Source Material
Use the current site and public-facing bio material as source reference:

- https://www.drive-fast-take-chances.com/
- https://www.linkedin.com/in/delahackerrocker/

If LinkedIn cannot be fetched directly, use the current site copy and any safely available public snippet/context, but **do not invent claims**.

## Key Facts to Preserve
The rewrite should preserve and foreground these facts where appropriate:

- Dela bridges design and engineering
- strong experience in UX, interface design, systems thinking, prototyping, and implementation
- five shipped Call of Duty releases
- work connected to XR / interactive projects involving Google, the NBA, and the U.S. Air Force
- background spans games, immersive products, tools, and experimental interactive work

Do **not** exaggerate or add titles, achievements, or clients that are not supported by the current public material.

## Core Writing Direction
Move the voice away from:
- overlong autobiographical framing
- repeated self-mythologizing language
- overly poetic or fantasy-coded language when it weakens clarity
- phrases that sound more like inner monologue than portfolio copy

Move the voice toward:
- senior
- clear
- concise
- credible
- hybrid design/engineering
- shipped work
- cross-disciplinary collaboration
- production awareness

## Specific Tone Guidance
The writing should feel like it belongs on a portfolio site for someone who:
- designs interfaces
- prototypes and implements
- works well between design and engineering
- has shipped real work
- understands production constraints

Avoid lines that feel like:
- “battle-hardened code warrior”
- “web-slinger”
- “magick”
- childhood origin-story copy
- self-romanticizing language that slows the reader down

Memorable voice is fine. Overwritten voice is not.

## Scope
Review and tighten the copy on the major public-facing pages/sections, especially:

- homepage hero
- homepage supporting text
- focus/summary cards
- about section/page
- selected work intro text
- interactive portfolio intro text
- Call of Duty page intro
- any repeated long-form explanatory text that appears before the work itself

## Rewrite Goals by Area

### 1. Homepage Hero
The hero should quickly answer:
- who this person is
- what they do
- why they are credible

It should foreground:
- UX / interface design
- design + engineering bridge
- shipped interactive work

### 2. Focus / Summary Areas
Replace vague or overly wordy phrasing with concise statements that clarify specialty.

### 3. About
Keep personality, but compress heavily.
The about section should communicate role, range, and point of view without becoming memoir.

### 4. Selected Work / Interactive Portfolio
Intro copy should get out of the way and let the work lead.
Short setup text is good. Long ramps are not.

### 5. Call of Duty Section
This section should clearly and confidently foreground:
- five shipped major Call of Duty releases
- UI/UX feature work
- technical design / implementation strength
- large-team / production-scale experience

Keep it strong and factual.

## What to Remove or Reduce
Reduce or remove:
- childhood origin-story material
- repeated “artist vs technologist” framing
- fantasy-flavored flourishes that weaken professionalism
- long explanatory paragraphs before actual work
- emotional filler lines that do not add useful information

## What to Keep
Keep:
- confidence
- some personality
- distinctive voice where it does not get in the way
- the sense that this is a hybrid creative/technical practitioner
- project-specific facts and shipped-work credibility

## Deliverables
Provide:

1. A concise audit of which sections/pages were rewritten
2. Paste-ready rewritten copy for each revised section
3. Brief notes on any copy that was intentionally left mostly intact
4. A short summary of the overall tone shift

## Implementation Preference
Prefer **direct replacement copy** over abstract recommendations.

That means:
- show the old section/page name
- provide the new tightened copy
- keep it ready to paste into the site

## Acceptance Criteria
This task is successful when:

1. The main site copy is noticeably tighter and easier to scan.
2. The rewritten text is more employer/client-facing.
3. The strongest facts are surfaced earlier.
4. The writing sounds senior and credible without becoming bland.
5. The work is allowed to lead instead of being buried under long setup copy.
6. No unsupported claims are introduced.
7. The result still feels like Dela, just sharper and more disciplined.

## Final Note
The goal is not to sterilize the voice.
The goal is to remove drag.

This rewrite should feel like the same person, but with better editorial judgment.
