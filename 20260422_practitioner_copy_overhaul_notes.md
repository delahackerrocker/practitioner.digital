## Tone decisions

- Led with one fast role sentence instead of stacking broad identity labels.
- Favored shipped work, product context, and contribution framing over self-descriptive adjectives.
- Kept the hybrid artist / engineer identity, but moved it into concrete language about UI/UX, systems, prototyping, and implementation.
- Reduced fantasy wording, stacked metaphors, and generalized "experience" language.
- Replaced vague portfolio filler with product nouns: extraction, squad continuity, visual systems, stealth communication, tablet-and-VR workflows.
- Used the Call of Duty cluster as the anchor for credibility, then let XR and original-IP work broaden the range.
- Tightened summaries so they explain why a recruiter or hiring lead should care in one quick scan.
- Preserved personality by keeping the work mix broad and the voice slightly sharp, but removed copy that felt try-hard or first-pass.
- Chose honesty over polish where the public record was thin, especially for older or lightly documented projects.
